The Basics

When: 47 days before Easter on the day before Ash Wednesday, so the date changes every year
Where: New Orleans (but Sydney does one now too!)
Why: To mark the start of the Christian holiday Lent

The iconic purple, green, and gold beaded necklaces that define Mardi Gras.

What's in a Name?

So "Mardi Gras" literally translates to "Fat Tuesday" in French, which is honestly quite fitting. Back in the day, it was all about eating up your goodies before giving them up for Lent ‚Äì you know, that Christian tradition where you fast from rich foods (like fats) from Ash Wednesday right through to Easter. Basically, this parade was a proper send-off before everyone had to behave themselves for 40 days. I mean, I'd definitely need a massive party if I was giving up all my favourite things too!

This tradition is so important because the French had a huge influence over this part of America back then ‚Äì which is also where all that incredible Creole cooking comes from. Lucky for us, these traditions stuck around! Interestingly, this Christian festival is celebrated all over the world. In England, we've got pancake day on the exact same day ‚Äì though I think New Orleans does it with a bit more flair, if I'm being honest.

What to Expect

Picture this: brass bands playing jazz music on every corner, the most gorgeous brightly coloured costumes you've ever seen, king cakes (seriously, try them), and of course those iconic beaded necklaces that everyone seems to collect by the dozen. It's basically a non-stop celebration of incredible food, brilliant music, and good drinks. What more could you want?

Traditional King Cake with purple, green, and gold icing ‚Äì a Mardi Gras must-try!

üí° Pro Tip: King Cakes

Don't leave New Orleans without trying a king cake! These sweet braided pastries are decorated in the traditional Mardi Gras colors and often contain a hidden plastic baby. If you find it in your slice, tradition says you have to buy the next cake!

Beyond New Orleans

Here's something that might surprise you ‚Äì Mardi Gras isn't even originally from New Orleans! The first American Mardi Gras was actually held in Mobile, Alabama. And now it's gone completely global. Sydney, Australia has their own version, which is absolutely massive. It's wild how this tradition has spread across the world!

"It's a true bucket list festival that everyone should experience at least once."

Should You Go?

If you're into proper southern food, amazing music, and just fancy a good boogie for a few days, then this place is absolutely made for you. Honestly, it's a true bucket list festival that everyone should experience at least once.

And if you've read our previous blogs about New Orleans, you'll already know just how stunning that city is. The buildings alone are absolutely breathtaking ‚Äì add in the Mardi Gras atmosphere and you've got yourself an unforgettable trip.
