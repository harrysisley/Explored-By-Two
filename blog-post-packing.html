Traveling with just carry-on luggage changed the way we explore the world. No more waiting at baggage carousels, no more lost luggage anxiety, and total freedom to move. Here's how we survived 3 months with just 7kg each.

Why We Switched to Carry-On Only

After dragging massive suitcases through the cobblestone streets of Europe, we knew something had to change. The freedom of having everything you need on your back is addictive. Plus, it saves money on airline fees and saves time at the airport.

Our 40L backpacks ready for adventure.

The Capsule Wardrobe Strategy

The key is a color-coordinated capsule wardrobe. Every top must match every bottom. We stick to neutrals (black, white, grey, navy) and add one accent color. Merino wool is your best friend‚Äîit's antimicrobial, temperature regulating, and doesn't hold odors.

Our Packing List Essentials:

  • Merino Wool T-shirts: 3x (wear one, pack two)
  • Convertible Pants: 1 pair that looks good in the city and on the trail
  • Lightweight Down Jacket: Packs into its own pocket
  • Solid Toiletries: Shampoo bars and solid deodorant save liquid allowance
  • Microfiber Towel: Dries fast and takes up no space

üí° Pro Tip: The "Ranger Roll"

Learn to "Ranger Roll" your clothes. It compresses them tighter than folding or regular rolling and prevents them from unrolling in your bag. You can fit 30% more stuff this way!

Tech and Gear

As digital nomads, we still need our tech. We share chargers where possible and use a multi-port GaN charger that's tiny but powerful. We ditched the heavy laptop for an iPad Pro and a lightweight MacBook Air.

The Result

We've never felt lighter. We can hop on a train, jump on a motorbike, or walk 5km to our hostel without breaking a sweat. Give it a try on your next weekend trip and see if you can scale it up!
